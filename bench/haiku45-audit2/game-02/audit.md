## Finding 1 — Grave-Eater attack damage/horror was assigned sequentially, hiding legal Leo soak
- Step/round: R3 Investigation, lines 135-148
- Severity: unclear
- Rule: RR “Dealing Damage/Horror”: determine the amount of damage and horror, assign all tokens, then apply them simultaneously; an asset may be assigned damage/horror up to the amount that would defeat it. Grave-Eater: “After Grave-Eater attacks you: Discard 1 card at random from your hand.” Leo De Luca has 2 health and 2 sanity.
- Evidence: Leo already had 1 damage and 1 horror from lines 111-118. Grave-Eater’s AoO at line 136 dealt 1 damage and 1 horror. The log assigned 1 damage to Leo, immediately discarded Leo at line 142, then offered a separate horror assignment where Wendy took the horror at lines 144-147. The 1 horror should still have been assignable to Leo during the same simultaneous assignment step.

## Finding 2 — Leo De Luca leaving play removed an extra remaining action after his bonus action was already used
- Step/round: R3 Investigation, lines 103-152
- Severity: unclear
- Rule: RR “Action” FAQ 1.10: if an investigator has additional actions, “the first action he or she takes that is able to qualify as that additional action automatically uses that additional action.” Leo De Luca: “You may take an additional action during your turn.”
- Evidence: Wendy began R3 with 4 actions from Leo at line 103. Her first action, Investigate Guest Hall, qualified for Leo’s unrestricted additional action and left 3 actions at line 131. After spending 1 action to move, the log showed 2 actions at line 137. When Leo was discarded during the AoO, the action count dropped to 1 at line 143 and remained 1 after the move at line 152. No rule or card effect caused Wendy to lose a standard action; Leo’s additional action had already been the first qualifying action of the turn.
