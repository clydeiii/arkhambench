## Finding 1 — The Necronomicon's Revelation never resolved when it was added to Daisy's hand; the weakness sat inert in hand until randomly discarded
- Step/round: R6 (log.md line 334, "Research Librarian found The Necronomicon", hand 5→6); consequences at R8 lines 441–444 and R11 line 614
- Severity: exploit
- Rule: RR "Revelation": "When a weakness card enters an investigator's hand, that investigator must immediately resolve all revelation abilities on the card as if it were just drawn." The Necronomicon (01009): "**Revelation** - Put The Necronomicon into play in your threat area, with 3 horror on it. It cannot leave play while it has 1 or more horror on it. Treat each [elder_sign] you reveal on a chaos token as a [auto_fail]."
- Evidence: Research Librarian's search added the weakness to hand at R6 (h5→h6, d14→13) with no Revelation resolution — it never entered the threat area with 3 horror. It then sat in hand as a dead card until R11 ("Grave-Eater's attack forced a random discard of The Necronomicon", line 614), permanently neutralizing Daisy's signature weakness — a card that "cannot leave play while it has 1 or more horror on it" left the game via a hand discard. The skip also had a direct test consequence: at R8 (lines 441–444) Daisy revealed an elder sign on Crypt Chill and resolved it as +0 success with a bonus draw; with The Necronomicon correctly in play the elder sign is an auto-fail, and failing Crypt Chill (01167) forces "choose and discard 1 asset you control." This is exploitable by any Daisy player: fetching the Necronomicon to hand defangs it for the entire game.

## Finding 2 — Daisy's Tome-only additional action was spent on the Study's location ability (a 2-action activation taken with only 1 standard action remaining)
- Step/round: R5 (log.md lines 283–291)
- Severity: exploit
- Rule: Daisy Walker (01002): "You may take an additional action during your turn, which can only be used on [[Tome]] [action] abilities." RR FAQ 1.10 ("additional actions"): the additional action can only be consumed by a qualifying action. RR Triggered Abilities: "An [action] triggered ability may be triggered … only if the player uses one action for each [action] specified in the ability's cost." Study: Aberrant Gateway (50013) is a location with no Tome trait: "[action] [action]: Draw 3 cards."
- Evidence: R5 turn: Move (line 269) and Investigate (line 275) consumed 2 of Daisy's 3 standard actions, leaving "2 actions left (1 Tome-only)" (line 283) — i.e. 1 standard + the Tome-restricted action. The engine then offered and executed "Study (Aberrant Gateway): spend 2 actions to draw 3 cards" (lines 285–291), paying one of those 2 actions with the Tome-only action on a non-Tome location ability. The option was illegal to offer (only 1 usable action for a 2-action cost) and its resolution gave Daisy an illegal action's worth of value (3 cards). Contrast R1, where the same ability was legally paid with 2 standard actions and the Tome-only action was spent on Old Book of Lore.

## Finding 3 — Research Librarian's optional reaction auto-resolved with no decision, force-fetching the signature weakness over a legal Tome target
- Step/round: R6 (log.md lines 331–335)
- Severity: unclear
- Rule: RR Triggered Abilities: "A [reaction] triggered ability with a specified triggering condition **may** be triggered any time that triggering condition is met." Research Librarian (01032): "[reaction] After Research Librarian enters play: Search your deck for a [[Tome]] asset and add it to your hand. Shuffle your deck."
- Evidence: Between "Spent 1 action: Play Research Librarian" (line 333) and "Research Librarian found The Necronomicon" (line 334) no decision was presented: the optional reaction was force-triggered and the engine itself selected the search target. The player got neither the choice to decline nor the choice of which Tome — and a legitimate target existed: the second copy of Medical Texts (Item. Tome.) was in the deck at that moment (copy 1 was discarded to hand size at R2 line 123 and stayed in the discard pile; copy 2 was drawn from the deck at R8 line 443, with no discard-reshuffle event in between). Every other search in the log (Old Book of Lore, mulligan) presents a decision; this one silently fetched the worst possible card. In this transcript the effect happened to favor the player only because of Finding 1 (the inert weakness later soaked a random discard); with correct Revelation handling this auto-fetch would have put the Necronomicon into play against the player's will.

## Finding 4 — Mulligan resolved one card at a time with replacements revealed between choices, instead of a single declaration
- Step/round: R1 (log.md lines 2–15)
- Severity: pro-player
- Rule: RR "Mulligan": "that player has a single opportunity to declare a mulligan on any number of the drawn cards he or she does not wish to keep in his or her starting hand. These cards are set aside, and an equivalent number of cards are drawn and added to the player's starting hand. The set-aside cards are then shuffled back."
- Evidence: The engine's flow ("Choose cards to mulligan (one at a time…)") let Daisy mulligan Scrying, see the replacement ("Mulliganed Scrying, drew Hyperawareness", line 5), and only then decide to mulligan Knife (line 8) with knowledge of the first replacement. RAW requires all set-aside cards to be declared in a single opportunity before any replacements are drawn; the sequential reveal grants information the rules do not allow. (Set-aside cards were correctly shuffled back afterward — deck 26→28 at line 17 — so only the decision structure is wrong.)
