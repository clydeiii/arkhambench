# Deck strategy notes (all five investigators)

Your deck is a fixed "Better Starter Deck" (community-designed, core-set pool). All five
were designed as a compatible set, so they vary in how well-suited they are to solo play
— playing to your investigator's actual strengths matters more than playing them all the
same way. `./ahlcg card <name>` shows any card's full text. Your signature weakness is
listed with each deck: it is shuffled into your deck and WILL come up; plan for it.

## Roland Banks (Guardian/Seeker) — flexible fighter-investigator
Roland fights and investigates equally well. You have four real weapons (.45 Automatic,
2× Machete, Roland's .38 Special) plus 2× Knife in a pinch; Physical Training boosts the
big swings. For clues: 2× Magnifying Glass and 2× Flashlight leverage his intellect 3,
Perception for hard tests, Deduction to double up, and his reaction (free clue at his
location after defeating an enemy — once per round) means killing enemies IS
investigating; Evidence! extends that. Dynamite Blast is the multi-enemy trump card —
fund it with Emergency Cache. Guard Dog deals passive damage while soaking. His real
weakness is 5 sanity: horror treacheries (Rotting Remains) can kill him long before
damage does — hold Guts, use Dodge to cancel the scariest attacks, First Aid only when
truly needed. Signature weakness: Cover Up (3 clues in your threat area; discover clues
at its location to clear it before the game ends or take 1 mental trauma).

## Daisy Walker (Seeker/Mystic) — the clue engine
Daisy's intellect 5 makes most investigations near-automatic; the deck is built to
convert that into tempo, not to fight. Get a Tome down fast — her extra action and elder
sign do nothing without one. Old Book of Lore is the priority (Research Librarian finds
it); Dr. Milan Christopher is the single best mulligan target (constant +1 intellect
plus resource income). Working a Hunch is a free clue. When enemies come: Mind over
Matter is her best card (use intellect in place of combat/agility for the round),
Blinding Light evades with willpower and chips 1 damage, Knife handles Swarm of Rats.
Ward of Protection cancels a nasty treachery's revelation outright for 1 resource and 1
horror — her sanity 9 can afford it. Her 5 health is tiny — do not tank hits. Signature weakness: The Necronomicon (enters
your threat area with 3 horror, eats a hand slot, and turns your elder signs into
autofails until you spend 3 actions clearing it — budget those actions).

## "Skids" O'Toole (Rogue/Guardian) — tempo fighter
Balanced stats with a combat lean. The .45 Automatic is the best weapon — prioritize it;
the .41 Derringer wants Beat Cop's +1 combat first so its succeed-by-2 bonus lands.
Overpower and Vicious Blow push attacks over the top; Opportunist wants tests you win by
3+ so it bounces back. Burglary only pays at shroud 0–1 locations. His agility 4 plus
Sneak Attack is a real kill line: evade, then burn the exhausted enemy for 2. Elusive
and On the Lam are escape hatches when a turn goes wrong. His ability (2 resources = an
extra action, once per turn) is the deck's engine — Emergency Cache fuels it. Treachery
willpower tests are his blind spot; save Guts. Signature weakness: Hospital Debts (bank
6 resources onto it — 2 per round max — before game end or lose 2 XP; start feeding it
early, not in a panic).

## Agnes Baker (Mystic/Survivor) — willpower violence
Agnes turns willpower 5 into combat. Shrivelling is the main weapon (fight at willpower,
+1 damage; Holy Rosary and Arcane Studies push it higher). The symbol-token horror it
risks is often UPSIDE: any horror on Agnes triggers her reaction (1 damage to an enemy
at her location, once per phase). Forbidden Knowledge is not an economy card in her
hands — it is a weapon: self-inflict 1 horror in any phase to snipe, including after a
hunter moves in and before it attacks. Route incoming horror to Holy Rosary and Arcane
Initiate soak so her sanity stays available as ammunition; Fearless refills it. Baseball
Bat is the backup plan (+2 combat covers her base 2) but breaks on skull/autofail.
Flashlight makes shroud ≤2 locations reliable; Drawn to the Flame is 2 fast clues if you
can stomach the extra encounter card. Signature weakness: Dark Memory (2 horror every
turn it sits in your hand — play it when the agenda can absorb 1 doom, or hold it only
when you can afford the bleed).

## Wendy Adams (Survivor/Rogue) — evasion and token defiance
Wendy trades cards for certainty: her reaction discards a card to cancel and redraw a
chaos token (once per test), so with cards in hand her effective stats run a point
higher than printed — taking marginal tests is correct for her in a way it isn't for
anyone else. Leo De Luca is the most valuable card in the deck: play him turn 1 if at
all possible (the extra action repays the 6 cost fast). Clues come from Flashlight,
Perception, "Look what I found!", and Lucky!. Her agility 4 plus Dig Deep, Survival
Instinct, and Manual Dexterity means evading, not killing, is the plan; Backstab (fight
at agility, 3 total damage) is her one real attack — save it for a must-kill; Cunning
Distraction is usually too expensive to play. Scavenging plus Wendy's Amulet is the
late-game engine: commit or discard Items freely, then recover them on a strong
investigate (Flashlight first) while the Amulet replays your best events from the
discard pile. Signature weakness: Abandoned and Alone (2 direct horror — no soak — and
your discard pile is removed from the game; it punishes hoarding your discard as a
resource, so use recursion early rather than banking it).
